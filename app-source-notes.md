# Answerable app source notes

Use this file as a source map for website articles and scheduled blog posts. Articles should quote or closely adapt Answerable app training content where possible, rather than inventing new theological material.

## Core product position

Answerable Faith is a Christian apologetics training app. It helps regular Christians, evangelical groups, small groups, youth/student ministries and church leaders practise clear, gracious answers before real conversations happen.

The app is not only an answer library. Its distinctive angle is practice:

- face a real question,
- try your own answer,
- compare with a model response,
- review Scripture and context,
- practise with realistic conversation partners,
- learn to answer with clarity, patience and grace.

## Editorial rules for app-led articles

- Use the app as the source map.
- Quote or closely adapt app wording where possible.
- Do not invent new theology.
- Do not overclaim historical, scientific or doctrinal points.
- Do not straw-man atheists, Muslims, Jewish readers, Jehovah’s Witnesses, deconstructing Christians or spiritual seekers.
- Make answers useful for regular Christians and evangelical groups.
- Include both truth and posture: what to answer and how to say it.
- Use Scripture references only where they are already in the app content or are directly relevant and handled carefully.
- Keep most sentences short.
- Use clear summaries near the top for AI/search systems.
- End with a natural app CTA: practise the answer in Answerable.

### Is the Trinity a contradiction?

Source app content:
- questions.json id g02
- conversations.json chain g02

Key app ideas:
- The pressure point is logic.
- A contradiction would be saying God is one person and three persons, or one being and three beings.
- Christianity says God is one in being or essence and three in person.
- The Father, Son and Spirit are personally distinct.
- They share the one divine nature fully and eternally.
- The Son is not a lesser god, the Spirit is not a force, and the Father is not merely one role God plays.
- Mystery is not the same as logical contradiction.

Useful app wording:
> There is no contradiction because Christianity does not say God is one and three in the same sense.

> A contradiction would be one God and three gods, or one person and three persons. Christianity is saying one being and three persons.

Scripture references used by the app/article:
- John 1:1
- 2 Corinthians 13:14
- 1 Timothy 2:5

### Did scribes change the New Testament?

Source app content:
- questions.json id b08
- conversations.json chain b08

Key app ideas:
- Scribes copied the New Testament by hand for centuries.
- Christians should not deny copying mistakes.
- We know about variants because manuscripts can be compared.
- Some variants are accidental: spelling, skipped words, repeated lines or word order.
- Some were intentional clarifications.
- The existence of variants is not the same as the loss of the text.
- Major Christian doctrines do not depend on disputed readings.

Useful app wording:
> They did make mistakes and occasional changes; the reason we know is that we can compare thousands of copies.

> The existence of variants is not the same as the loss of the text. It is the evidence by which the text can be examined.

Scripture references used by the app/article:
- Luke 1:1
- 1 Corinthians 15:3
- Isaiah 40:8

### Could the disciples have hallucinated Jesus?

Source app content:
- questions.json id r03
- conversations.json chain r03

Key app ideas:
- Grief can affect people deeply.
- Do not answer by saying hallucinations never happen.
- The issue is whether hallucination explains the whole resurrection witness.
- Hallucinations are private experiences.
- The resurrection testimony involves multiple people, groups, physical interaction, an empty tomb and a transformed public movement.
- The disciples claimed Jesus appeared bodily, spoke with them, ate with them and showed His wounds.

Useful app wording:
> Hallucination does not explain the resurrection evidence. Hallucinations are private experiences; the resurrection testimony involves multiple people, groups, physical interaction, an empty tomb, and a transformed public movement.

> The best explanation of the apostolic witness is the one they gave: God raised Jesus from the dead.

Scripture references used by the app/article:
- Luke 24:39
- 1 Corinthians 15:6
- Acts 1:3

### How should Christians answer slavery in the Bible?

Source app content:
- questions.json id e02
- conversations.json chain e02

Key app ideas:
- This question needs moral clarity and care.
- Humans have used Scripture wickedly to defend evil.
- Human beings made in God’s image must never be treated as property.
- Regulation is not the same as moral endorsement.
- Christians should plainly condemn race-based chattel slavery as sin.
- The Gospel does not dehumanize people; it redeems them.
- Do not dodge, defend evil or flatten the ancient world.

Useful app wording:
> Slavery in the Bible must be answered carefully because the word covers different realities, and because humans have used Scripture wickedly to defend evil.

> The first note should be moral clarity: human beings made in God’s image must never be treated as property.

> Christians should plainly condemn race-based chattel slavery as sin. The Gospel does not dehumanize people; it redeems them.

Scripture references used by the app/article:
- Philemon 1:16
- Matthew 7:12
- Galatians 3:28
