## is-the-trinity-a-contradiction

**Question ID:** `g02`  
**Category:** `god`  
**Difficulty:** hard  
**Related categories:** god, christ, bible  
**Follow-up ID:** `g12`

### Question

How can one God be Father, Son, and Spirit without contradiction?

### Answer

There is no contradiction because Christianity does not say God is one and three in the same sense. A contradiction would be saying God is one person and three persons, or one being and three beings. The Christian claim is different: God is one in being or essence and three in person.

The Father, Son, and Spirit are personally distinct. The Father sends, the Son is sent, and the Spirit is given. Yet they are not separate beings. They share the one divine nature fully and eternally. This means the Son is not a lesser god, the Spirit is not a force, and the Father is not merely one role God plays.

The Trinity is above our full comprehension, but it is not logical nonsense. It is precise language drawn from the whole Bible’s witness to the one God who reveals Himself as Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

### Tone training
- **Start here:** Clarify the meaning of contradiction: one God in being, three persons, not one person and three persons in the same sense.
- **Say this gently:** The Trinity is mysterious because God is greater than creation, but mystery is not the same as logical contradiction.
- **Avoid this trap:** Do not pretend the Trinity is easy or reduce it to a slogan.
- **Ask this follow-up:** Are you objecting to the doctrine itself, or to a mistaken version where Christians believe in three gods?

### Confidence
- **Level:** high
- **Note:** The logical distinction between being and person is standard orthodox Trinitarian theology.

## did-scribes-change-the-new-testament

**Question ID:** `b08`  
**Category:** `bible`  
**Difficulty:** hard  
**Related categories:** bible, tactics, contradictions  
**Follow-up ID:** `b16`

### Question

How should I answer claims that scribes made copying errors in the New Testament text?

### Answer

Scribes did make copying errors, but that does not mean the New Testament message was corrupted. The manuscript evidence is broad enough for those errors and variants to be detected.

Some variants were accidental: spelling, skipped words, repeated lines, or word order. Some were intentional clarifications. But textual criticism compares manuscripts across regions and centuries to recover the earliest attainable wording. The major Christian doctrines do not depend on disputed readings. The deity of Christ, the cross, resurrection, Trinity, salvation by grace, and call to holiness are found throughout undisputed passages.

The existence of variants is not the same as the loss of the text. It is the evidence by which the text can be examined.

### Tone training
- **Start here:** Begin by acknowledging scribes copied by hand and variants exist.
- **Say this gently:** Christian confidence is not based on pretending variants do not exist; it is based on the ability to compare many witnesses.
- **Avoid this trap:** Do not say “nothing changed” if you mean “no doctrine was lost.”
- **Ask this follow-up:** Which variant do you think changes Christian belief?

### Confidence
- **Level:** high
- **Note:** The manuscript evidence supports textual reliability, while specific variants require careful discussion.

## could-the-disciples-have-hallucinated-jesus

**Question ID:** `r03`  
**Category:** `resurrection`  
**Difficulty:** hard  
**Related categories:** resurrection, christ, philosophy, bible  
**Follow-up ID:** `r04`

### Question

Could the disciples have hallucinated the resurrection?

### Answer

Hallucination does not explain the resurrection evidence. Hallucinations are private experiences; the resurrection testimony involves multiple people, groups, physical interaction, an empty tomb, and a transformed public movement.

The disciples did not merely feel that Jesus was spiritually alive. They claimed He appeared bodily, spoke with them, ate with them, and showed His wounds. Paul says Jesus appeared to more than five hundred people, many still alive when he wrote, which invites checking. Hallucination also does not explain why enemies such as Paul changed, or why the tomb was empty in Jerusalem where the message began.

The best explanation of the apostolic witness is the one they gave: God raised Jesus from the dead.

### Tone training
- **Start here:** Begin by showing hallucinations do not fit the full set of facts.
- **Say this gently:** Hallucinations do not explain the empty tomb, group appearances, enemy conversions, and the disciples’ transformed proclamation.
- **Avoid this trap:** Do not claim hallucinations never happen; explain why this case does not fit that explanation.
- **Ask this follow-up:** How would hallucination explain both the empty tomb and appearances to groups and skeptics?

### Confidence
- **Level:** medium
- **Note:** The hallucination hypothesis is weak historically, but resurrection apologetics involves historical judgment.

## how-should-christians-answer-slavery-in-the-bible

**Question ID:** `e02`  
**Category:** `ethics`  
**Difficulty:** hard  
**Related categories:** ethics, bible, tactics, posture  
**Follow-up ID:** `t02`

### Question

How do I answer when someone raises slavery in the Bible?

### Answer

Slavery in the Bible must be answered carefully because the word covers different realities, and because humans have used Scripture wickedly to defend evil. The first note should be moral clarity: human beings made in God’s image must never be treated as property.

The Bible’s world included debt-service, household servitude, foreign slavery after war, and brutal forms of bondage. Old Testament law does not present these as Edenic ideals; it addresses a fallen social world, restrains abuse, condemns kidnapping, requires rest, limits permanent debt-servitude among Israelites, and gives servants legal protections unusual in the ancient world. Those regulations are not the same as endorsing race-based chattel slavery.

The New Testament does not launch a political revolution by the sword, but it transforms the moral foundation: masters and servants stand under one Lord, and in Christ social superiority is shattered. Christians who used Scripture to defend man-stealing slavery were contradicting the Bible’s own condemnation of kidnapping and its doctrine of human dignity.

Christians should plainly condemn race-based chattel slavery as sin. The Gospel does not dehumanize people; it redeems them and calls believers to justice, repentance, and love.

### Tone training
- **Start here:** Start by taking the moral concern seriously and refusing to defend human exploitation.
- **Say this gently:** The Bible’s story moves toward human dignity in Christ, even while addressing ancient social realities in a fallen world.
- **Avoid this trap:** Do not sound like you are minimizing slavery or defending racial slavery.
- **Ask this follow-up:** Which passage are you thinking of: Old Testament law, Paul’s letters, or the history of Christian slaveholding?

### Confidence
- **Level:** careful
- **Note:** The biblical and historical issues are complex and must be handled with moral clarity and nuance.
