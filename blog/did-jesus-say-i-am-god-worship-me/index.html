Did Jesus Say “I Am God; Worship Me”?

The exact modern sentence is absent, but Jesus’ claim to divine identity is clear in what He said, did and received.

Published 20 July 2026 · Updated 20 July 2026

Practise in the app Back to blog
Answer in brief

Jesus did not use the exact modern sentence “I am God; worship me.” Yet He claimed divine identity by forgiving sins, using God’s “I am” language, sharing the Father’s honour and glory, and receiving worship. His hearers understood the force of those claims, and the New Testament presents Him as truly divine.

A person can make a clear claim without using the one sentence we demand. The right question is not whether Jesus used modern debate language. It is whether His words and actions would be proper for anyone less than God. The Gospels give a strong answer.

How did Jesus claim divine authority?

Jesus forgave sins in Mark 2:5–12. The teachers of the law asked, “Who can forgive sins but God alone?” Jesus then healed the man to show that the Son of Man had authority to forgive. He was not merely announcing that God forgave someone. He acted with God’s own authority.

He also said that all people should honour the Son just as they honour the Father (John 5:23). In John 8:58 He declared, “Before Abraham was, I am.” His listeners did not hear that as a harmless claim to age. They reached for stones because they understood its divine weight.

Did Jesus receive worship?

Yes. After Jesus walked on the sea, His disciples worshipped Him (Matthew 14:33). After the resurrection, Thomas addressed Him as “My Lord and my God” (John 20:28). Jesus did not rebuke him. This matters because faithful servants of God refuse worship when it is wrongly offered to them.

Jesus also spoke of the glory He shared with the Father before the world existed (John 17:5). Taken together, His claims concern divine names, works, honour and glory—not merely a special role as a prophet.

What is the clearest way to answer?

Be honest about the wording. Jesus did not say that exact English sentence. Then move to the evidence: He forgave sins, used divine language, received worship and accepted Thomas’s confession. This avoids pretending every verse is equally direct while still taking the whole Gospel witness seriously.

How to say it gently

You might say: “Jesus didn’t use that exact modern phrase. But He did things that belong to God, spoke with divine authority and received divine honour. Could we look together at what He says in John 5, John 8 and John 20?”

What not to say

Don’t mock the question or claim that one verse ends every discussion. Don’t assume a Muslim, sceptic or seeker knows the Christian meaning of Son of God. Explain the evidence with patience, and let the person read the passages in context.

The aim is not to win with a slogan. It is to help someone see why Christians confess Jesus as Lord and God.
