Is the Trinity a contradiction?

The Trinity can sound like a contradiction at first, but the Christian claim is not that God is one and three in the same sense.

Practise in the app Back to blog

Answer in brief: Christianity does not say God is one person and three persons, or one being and three beings. It says God is one in being or essence and three in person. The doctrine is mysterious, but it is not the same as saying one equals three in the same sense.

Answer in brief
  • A contradiction would use the same word in the same sense.
  • The Christian claim is one being and three persons.
  • Mystery is not the same as logical nonsense.

Many people hear the word Trinity and think Christians believe something like, “God is one God and three gods.” That would be a contradiction. It would also be a misunderstanding of the doctrine.

The public Answerable source frames the question carefully: how can one God be Father, Son and Spirit without contradiction? The answer begins by clarifying what Christianity is, and is not, claiming.

Start with the meaning of contradiction

A contradiction says the same thing is true and false in the same way at the same time. So “God is one person and three persons” would be contradictory. “God is one being and three persons” is a different claim.

That distinction does not make the Trinity simple. It does remove a common caricature. Christians are not saying there are three separate divine beings. We are saying the Father, Son and Spirit share the one divine nature fully and eternally.

“The Christian claim is different: God is one in being or essence and three in person.”
Answerable public source · question card g02

What Christians actually mean

The Father, Son and Holy Spirit are personally distinct. The Father sends, the Son is sent, and the Spirit is given. Yet they are not separate gods or parts of God.

This also protects Christians from two opposite mistakes. The Son is not a lesser god. The Spirit is not merely a force. The Father is not just one role that God plays for a while.

Christianity speaks this way because of the whole Bible’s witness to the one God who reveals Himself as Father, Son and Holy Spirit.

What not to say

Do not pretend the Trinity is easy. It is not. Christians should not reduce it to a slogan or act as if every mystery has disappeared.

At the same time, do not say, “God is beyond logic, so contradictions do not matter.” That creates a bigger problem. The better answer is more careful: God is greater than creation, but mystery is not the same as contradiction.

Scripture to keep in view

The Answerable source highlights three passages that help explain why Christians speak this way:

  • “The Word was with God, and the Word was God.” — John 1:1
  • “The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ and the love of God and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with you all.” — 2 Corinthians 13:14
  • “There is one God.” — 1 Timothy 2:5

How to say it gently

You might say, “I agree that one God and three gods would be a contradiction. But Christians are not saying that. We are saying God is one in being and three in person.”

That answer is clear without sounding smug. It also gives your friend room to say whether they object to the doctrine itself, or to a mistaken version where Christians believe in three gods.

FAQ

Does the Trinity mean Christians believe in three gods?

No. Christianity teaches one God, not three gods. The distinction is one being and three persons.

Is the Trinity easy to understand?

No. The Answerable source warns against pretending it is easy. The point is that mystery is not the same thing as contradiction.

What question should I ask next?

Ask whether the person objects to the doctrine itself, or to a mistaken version where Christians believe in three gods.
