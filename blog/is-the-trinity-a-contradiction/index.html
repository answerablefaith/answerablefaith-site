Is the Trinity a contradiction?

The Trinity can sound confusing, but Christians are not using “one” and “three” in the same way.

Practise in the appBack to blog

Answer in brief: The Trinity is not a contradiction because Christianity does not say God is one person and three persons. It says God is one in being, and three in person. That is mysterious, but it is not the same as logical nonsense.

Answer in brief
  • A contradiction uses the same word in the same sense.
  • The Christian claim is one being and three persons.
  • Mystery is not the same as logical nonsense.

Many readers ask this because the language sounds strange at first. That is understandable. The best first step is to slow down and ask what Christians are actually claiming.

This is one of several Christian apologetics questions where definitions matter. A careful answer should be clear enough to help, but humble enough to admit that God is greater than our full understanding.

Start with the meaning of contradiction

A contradiction says the same thing is true and false in the same way at the same time. “One being and three persons” is not the same claim as “one person and three persons.”

“The Christian claim is different: God is one in being or essence and three in person.”
Answerable public source · question card g02

What Christians actually mean

The Father, Son and Holy Spirit are personally distinct, yet they are not separate divine beings. That is why Christians use careful language about being and person.

If someone wants to keep exploring, it may help to read about Bible reliability questions, because the doctrine comes from reading the whole Bible together.

What not to say

Do not pretend the Trinity is simple. It is not. Also avoid saying that contradictions do not matter. A better answer is that mystery is not the same as contradiction.

Scripture to keep in view

  • “The Word was with God, and the Word was God.” — John 1:1
  • “The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ and the love of God and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with you all.” — 2 Corinthians 13:14
  • “There is one God.” — 1 Timothy 2:5

How to say it gently

You might say, “I agree that this would be a contradiction if Christians meant one person and three persons in the same sense. But that is not the claim. The claim is one being and three persons.”

That answer gives the other person room to respond. You can also practise this answer in Answerable before the conversation.

FAQ

Does the Trinity mean Christians believe in three gods?

No. Christianity teaches one God. The distinction is one being and three persons.

Is the Trinity easy to understand?

No. The point is that mystery is not the same thing as contradiction.

What question should I ask next?

Ask whether the person objects to the doctrine itself, or to a mistaken version of it.
