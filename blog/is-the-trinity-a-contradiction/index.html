Doctrine of God · Trinity

Is the Trinity a contradiction?

The Trinity can sound confusing, but Christians are not saying God is one and three in the same way.

Practise in the app Back to blog
Answer in brief

The Trinity is not a contradiction. Christianity does not say God is one person and three persons. It says God is one in being and three in person. That is a mystery, but it is not nonsense.

This is a fair question. The words “one God” and “three persons” can sound strange at first. Christians should not act as if the doctrine is easy.

But hard to understand is not the same as impossible or false. The first step is to ask what Christians actually mean.

Start with what contradiction means

A contradiction says the same thing is true and false in the same way at the same time.

For example, it would be a contradiction to say God is one person and three persons in the same sense. But that is not the Christian claim.

“The Christian claim is different: God is one in being or essence and three in person.”
Answerable public source · question card g02

What Christians actually mean

Christians believe there is one God. The Father is God. The Son is God. The Holy Spirit is God.

But the Father is not the Son. The Son is not the Spirit. The Spirit is not the Father. They are personally distinct, yet not three separate gods.

That is why Christians use careful words. God is one divine being. The Father, Son and Holy Spirit are three persons.

Mystery is not the same as contradiction

The Trinity is more than we can fully grasp. That should not surprise us. God is not small enough to fit inside our minds.

Still, Christians are not throwing logic away. We are making a careful claim: one being, three persons.

How to say it gently

You might say, “I understand why it sounds confusing. It would be a contradiction if Christians meant one person and three persons in the same sense. But the claim is one being and three persons.”

Then give the other person room to ask what they mean. They may be objecting to a mistaken version of the doctrine.

What not to say

Do not pretend the Trinity is simple. It is not. Do not say logic does not matter. It does.

Also be careful with illustrations. Many examples make God sound like one person wearing three masks, or like three parts added together. Those are not what Christians mean.

FAQ

Is the Trinity a contradiction?

No. Christianity says God is one in being and three in person. It does not say God is one person and three persons.

Does the Trinity mean Christians believe in three gods?

No. Christianity teaches one God, not three gods.

Is the Trinity easy to understand?

No. It is a mystery. But mystery is not the same as contradiction.

What question should I ask next?

Ask whether the person objects to the doctrine itself or to a mistaken version of it.
