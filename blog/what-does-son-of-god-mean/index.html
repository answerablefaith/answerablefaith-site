What Does Son of God Mean?

The title doesn’t describe a physical birth; it tells us who Jesus is in relation to the Father.

Published 17 July 2026 · Updated 17 July 2026

Practise in the app Back to blog
Answer in brief

“Son of God” doesn’t mean God had a physical child. Christians mean that Jesus is the eternal Son who shares the Father’s divine nature, reveals Him perfectly and comes as the promised King.

You can clear up much of the confusion by starting with what Christians don’t mean. The title isn’t biological language. It doesn’t suggest that God took a wife or produced a child. Christians reject that idea too.

Does “Son” mean Jesus was created?

Human sons begin after their fathers, but the Bible doesn’t place Jesus inside creation. John says the Word was with God and was God “in the beginning”. He then says all things were made through Him.

This is why the Nicene Creed says the Son is “begotten, not made”. The wording protects a simple truth: the Son is eternally from the Father, not a creature made by the Father. Bethlehem is when the eternal Son became human. It isn’t when He began to exist.

Is “Son of God” only another name for Messiah?

The title does carry royal meaning. Israel’s promised king was called God’s son. Jesus is that promised Messiah. Yet the New Testament goes further. The Son shares the Father’s glory, does the Father’s works and reveals the Father in a way no mere prophet can.

John 1 calls Him the Word who was God and became flesh. In John 14, Jesus says that whoever has seen Him has seen the Father. Christians aren’t saying a man climbed up into deity. They’re saying God the Son came down in grace.

Keep both truth and respect together

For some people, especially Muslim friends, the phrase can sound crude or offensive before the real Christian claim is heard. Don’t mock that concern. Agree that a physical reading would be wrong, then explain eternal relationship, divine identity and revelation. Clarity serves the truth, and respect serves the person.

How to explain it gently

You could say: “Christians don’t mean that God had a physical child. We mean that Jesus is eternally the Son. He shares the Father’s divine nature and shows us the Father perfectly. The title also names Him as God’s promised King.”

What not to say

Don’t say, “You’re taking it too literally.” That can dismiss a sincere concern. Don’t say Jesus became the Son at His birth, because the New Testament presents the Son as existing before Bethlehem. And don’t reduce the title to “special prophet”; that leaves out the claim the Gospels make about His divine identity.

Once the meaning is clear, move from the title to the person. Ask what your friend makes of Jesus Himself. The aim isn’t to win a definition. It’s to help someone see the Christian claim that God has made Himself known in His Son.
